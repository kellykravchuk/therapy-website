My Approach

I work integratively, with a core in psychodynamic and relational therapy, informed by attachment science, trauma‑informed care, and developmental frameworks (e.g., DIR/Floortime for children and families). We’ll explore how past relationships live in the present, including how an inner critic or old alarm system shows up today — then pair insight with skills for regulation, boundaries, and connection.

  • Warm, collaborative pace — safety before depth.
  • Active use of the here‑and‑now relationship (transference/countertransference) for growth.
  • Strengths‑forward: updating protective strategies that no longer fit.

About Kelly

Hi, I’m Kelly Kravchuk, MSW, ACSW (ASW133376) (she/her), a therapist serving the South Bay and clients across California. I integrate psychodynamic and relational therapy with attachment and trauma‑informed care. My background includes work with neurodivergent youth and families through parent coaching and developmental support, and short‑term counseling with adults in a college setting.

Clinically, I’m interested in how early relationships live on inside of us and show up in the here‑and‑now. I use formulation to make sense of patterns like self‑criticism or people‑pleasing, then pair insight with practical skills for regulation, boundaries, and more authentic connection.

Outside the therapy room: I love cycling, scuba diving, traveling, and occasional oil painting — practices that keep me curious, grounded, and connected to joy.
